Scriptures & Sacred Excerpts

Explore the timeless wisdom of the Vedas, Upanishads, Bhagavad Gita, Puranas, Agamas, and Tamil spiritual literature. Each excerpt is accompanied by transliteration, translation, and commentary to aid contemplation and study.

  • Rigveda — Agni Sukta (Mandala 1.1)
    The First Hymn of the Rigveda
    Read ▼

    The Rigveda opens with a hymn to Agni, the divine fire. Agni is the mediator between humanity and the gods, carrying offerings and prayers to the celestial realms.

    Sanskrit:
    अग्निमीळे पुरोहितं यज्ञस्य देवम् ऋत्विजम् ।
    होतारं रत्नधातमम् ॥

    Translation:
    I praise Agni, the divine priest of the sacrifice, the invoker and bestower of treasures.

    Insight:
    Agni symbolizes the inner flame of knowledge and spiritual transformation.

  • Brihadaranyaka Upanishad
    Prayer for Enlightenment
    Read ▼

    One of the oldest Upanishads, exploring the nature of the Self and ultimate reality.

    Verse:
    असतो मा सद्गमय ।
    तमसो मा ज्योतिर्गमय ।
    मृत्योर्मामृतं गमय ॥

    Translation:
    Lead me from the unreal to the Real, from darkness to Light, from death to Immortality.

    Commentary:
    A profound prayer expressing the soul's yearning for truth, wisdom, and liberation.

  • Taittiriya Upanishad
    Invocation for Sacred Learning
    Read ▼

    A prayer traditionally recited before the study of the Vedas seeking blessings and clarity.

    Sanskrit:
    शं नो मित्रः शं वरुणः ।
    शं नो भवत्वर्यमा ।
    शं न इन्द्रो बृहस्पति।
    शं नो विष्णुरुरुक्रमः ॥

    :
    May the cosmic powers bless us with harmony, wisdom, strength, and protection.

Gayatri Mantra

Om Bhur Bhuvaḥ Swaḥ, Tat Savitur Vareṇyaṃ, Bhargo Devasya Dhīmahi, Dhiyo Yo Naḥ Prachodayāt

"We meditate on the divine light of Savitar, may it inspire our intellect."

Maha Mrityunjaya Mantra

Om Tryambakam Yajamahe Sugandhim Pushtivardhanam, Urvarukamiva Bandhanan Mrityor Mukshiya Maamritat

"We worship the three-eyed one (Lord Shiva) who nourishes all beings; may He liberate us from death and grant us immortality."

: Waves of Beauty

Author: Sri Adi Shankara Verses: 103 (in two parts)

A magnificent hymn celebrating the divine feminine energy through the transcendent beauty of the Goddess. This profound spiritual work reveals the interplay between Shiva and Shakti, exploring the mysteries of creation and consciousness through poetic devotion.

Kanaka Thaara Stotram

கனகதாரா ஸ்தோத்ரம்

: Golden Shower of Grace

Author: Sri Adi Shankara Verses: 18

A powerful invocation to Lakshmi Devi, the divine mother of prosperity and grace. This sacred hymn describes an abundant shower of golden blessings, symbolizing both material and spiritual wealth that flows from the compassion of the divine mother.

Shiva Vakkiyam

சிவவாக்கியம்

: Shiva's Grace

Author: Shiva Vakkiyae Verses: 500 verses

A profound collection of mystical verses by the Siddhar Sivavakkiyar, challenging ritualistic practices and emphasizing inner realization of the Divine. Through powerful poetry and spiritual wisdom, this timeless work explores self-knowledge, universal truth, and the path to liberation beyond caste, creed, and outward forms of worship.

Shiva Panchakshara Stotram

ஸிவ பஞ்சாக்ஷர ஸ்தோத்ரம்

: Hymn of the Five Syllables

Author: Sri Adi Shankara Verses: 5 + Phalashruti

A powerful and concise hymn glorifying Lord Shiva through the sacred Panchakshara mantra "Namah Shivaya". Each verse corresponds to one syllable, describing the divine attributes of the Lord.
