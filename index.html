Scriptures & Excerpts

Browse selected passages and short commentaries.

  • Rigveda — Mandala 1.1
    Hymn to Agni — Excerpt & transliteration
    Read ▼

    Sanskrit Excerpt:
    अग्निमीळे पुरोहितं यज्ञस्य देवम् ऋत्विजम् ।
    होतारं रत्नधातमम् ॥

    Transliteration:
    agnim īḷe purohitaṃ yajñasya devaṃ ṛtvijam
    hotāraṃ ratnadhātamam

    Meaning: I praise Agni, the household priest, the divine minister of the sacrifice, the hotṛ (invoker), bestower of treasures.

  • Brihadaranyaka Upanishad — I
    On truth and the self — clear commentary
    Read ▼

    Excerpt: "Asato mā sad gamaya, tamaso mā jyotir gamaya, mṛtyor mā amṛtaṃ gamaya."

    Translation: Lead me from the unreal to the real, from darkness to light, from death to immortality.

    Commentary: This verse is a prayer for inner awakening, where the self moves beyond illusion, ignorance, and mortality towards truth, knowledge, and eternal being.

  • Taittiriya Upanishad — Shiksha
    Guidelines for recitation and pronunciation
    Read ▼

    Excerpt:
    शं नो मित्रः शं वरुणः ।
    शं नो भवत्वर्यमा ।
    शं न इन्द्रो बृहस्पति: ।
    शं नो विष्णुरुरुक्रमः ॥

    Translation: May Mitra be gracious to us, may Varuṇa be gracious. May Aryaman be gracious, may Indra and Bṛhaspati be gracious. May Vishnu of wide strides be gracious.

    Context: The Shiksha Valli emphasizes correct recitation, humility, and blessings from the cosmic powers for clarity and wisdom.

Gayatri Mantra

Om Bhur Bhuvaḥ Swaḥ, Tat Savitur Vareṇyaṃ, Bhargo Devasya Dhīmahi, Dhiyo Yo Naḥ Prachodayāt

"We meditate on the divine light of Savitar, may it inspire our intellect."

Maha Mrityunjaya Mantra

Om Tryambakam Yajamahe Sugandhim Pushtivardhanam, Urvarukamiva Bandhanan Mrityor Mukshiya Maamritat

"We worship the three-eyed one (Lord Shiva) who nourishes all beings; may He liberate us from death and grant us immortality."

Soundarya Lahiri

ஸௌந்தர்ய லஹரீ

Meaning: Waves of Beauty

Author: Sri Adi Shankara Verses: 103 (in two parts)

A magnificent hymn celebrating the divine feminine energy through the transcendent beauty of the Goddess. This profound spiritual work reveals the interplay between Shiva and Shakti, exploring the mysteries of creation and consciousness through poetic devotion.

Kanaka Thaara Stotram

கனகதாரா ஸ்தோத்ரம்

Meaning: Golden Shower of Grace

Author: Sri Adi Shankara Verses: 18

A powerful invocation to Lakshmi Devi, the divine mother of prosperity and grace. This sacred hymn describes an abundant shower of golden blessings, symbolizing both material and spiritual wealth that flows from the compassion of the divine mother.

Two Sacred Verses, One Divine Truth

Both hymns, composed by the great philosopher Sri Adi Shankara, represent the eternal dialogue between the masculine and feminine divine. Soundarya Lahiri celebrates Shakti's transcendent beauty, while Kanaka Thaara Stotram invokes Lakshmi's abundant grace. Together, they illuminate the path to spiritual awakening.
