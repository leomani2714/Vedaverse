ஸ்ரீ ருत्रम् - Śatarudrīya

The Śatarudrīya (शतरुद्रीय) is a series of litanies devoted to the god Rudra (Shiva), found in the Yajurveda. It is one of the most important Vedic hymns dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Structure: The Rudram consists of two main parts:

  • Namakam (नमकम्): The first part containing invocations and salutations to Rudra in various forms
  • Chamakam (चमकम्): The second part containing prayers and requests for blessings, prosperity, and protection

Significance: Rudram is chanted during major Vedic rituals and is believed to bring protection, health, and spiritual progress. It is revered across Hindu traditions as one of the most powerful mantras in the Vedas, capable of removing fear and bestowing divine grace.

Benefits of Chanting: Regular chanting of Rudram is said to purify the mind, protect from negative forces, promote health and longevity, and lead to spiritual liberation.
